The Opportunity
From 11.7k to 50k organic visits/month
Trilogy Care is Australia's #3 Support at Home provider but 75% of organic traffic is branded and 69% hits the homepage. The substrate for compounding growth is in place — what's missing is non-branded discovery and AI-search visibility.

The 38,351-visit gap, four ways

Today: ~11,649 organic visits/month vs Right at Home's 22,914. Authority Score sits at 28, with 247 indexed pages and a near-clean technical foundation (Site Health 95%). Non-branded traffic is accelerating +17% MoM but still only 15% of the mix. Zero AI citations. Zero location pages. The 12-month target — 50,000 visits/month at 70% non-branded — closes by stacking four workstreams: technical + E-E-A-T quick wins, money pillars (Self-Managed, Costs & Funding), programmatic content (locations, comparisons, glossary), and authority building.

Foundation
Stack + the YMYL author-schema fix
The Next.js + Storyblok stack means content edits don't need a code deploy — bottleneck is review workflow, not engineering. The one critical foundation gap is the author schema for medical content.

Stack & staging

Framework: Next.js (App Router)
CMS: Storyblok (headless) — articles at /posts/<slug>
CDN / origin: Cloudflare → Vercel
Analytics: GA4, Search Console, GTM, Klaviyo
Staging: per-PR Vercel preview URLs (auto, with x-robots-tag: noindex) — no separate staging. hostname needed.

E-E-A-T fix (Phase 1, critical for YMYL)

Article JSON-LD currently emits "author": {"@type": "Organization"} — no named human, no clinical reviewer. For medical content (falls, medication, exercise), Google rater guidelines and AI search systems both downweight this. Fix: add author + reviewer fields to the Storyblok post content type, emit Person schema with sameAs links, create Person stories for Imran, Nick, Steve + Romy Blacklaw as the clinical reviewer.
